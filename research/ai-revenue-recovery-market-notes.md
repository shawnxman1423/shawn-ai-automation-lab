# AI revenue recovery / lead reactivation market notes

Short public summary of the AI lead-reactivation category.

## Category names

This workflow appears under several names:

- lead reactivation
- database reactivation
- dormant lead recovery
- stale lead revival
- pipeline recovery
- revenue recovery
- win-back automation
- aged lead reactivation

## What the agent does

```text
old leads / missed calls / unfinished estimates / inactive customers
→ AI voice, SMS, WhatsApp, or email follow-up
→ qualify intent
→ book appointment or hand off to human
→ update CRM and attribution
```

## Strategic read

The narrow category is young and crowded. The stronger wedge is not “AI SMS bot,” but:

```text
AI Revenue Recovery Engine for a specific vertical
```

Best vertical candidates:

1. Home services / contractors — missed calls, old estimates, seasonal reactivation.
2. Dental / medspa / clinics — unscheduled treatment, no-shows, old consults.
3. Insurance / mortgage / finance — aged leads and renewal triggers, with heavier compliance.
4. Real estate / seller leads — circumstances change; old leads can become relevant again.
5. B2B SaaS closed-lost pipeline — higher ACV but harder integrations and signal requirements.

## Compliance notes

Keep the AI bounded to:

- re-engagement
- qualification
- scheduling
- reminders
- CRM updates
- human handoff

Avoid letting the AI give regulated advice, make medical/financial/legal claims, or contact leads without consent.

## Positioning suggestion

```text
Recover revenue from leads and customers you already paid for.
```

More specific:

```text
AI revenue recovery for [vertical]: missed calls, stale leads, no-shows, old estimates, and inactive customers.
```
